VARobot Project Group

The University of Paderborn has a reputation of being one of the most innovative and technology-oriented universities in Germany. Therefore, as part of the requirements for completing the master of Computer Science degree program at the university, every student has to undertake a project. The project lasts for a duration of one year.
The name VARobot simply stands for “Virtual and Augmented Reality Assisted Robot Programming”. VARobot is one of the project groups in the focus area of Software Engineering. The main purpose of the VARobot project group is to extend the SPEARED(Simple Programming Environment in Augmented Reality with Enhanced Debugging) framework which was first developed by Michael Wieneke and Jonas Eilers during their master’s theses.



VARobot Project Team

The VARobot project group is supervised by two members of the SICP(Software Innovation Campus Paderborn). There are seven members in the project group and each of the seven members have been assigned particular roles. This is to ensure that there is clarity in terms of the responsibility each member has to fulfill.
As part of the process of extending the SPEARED framework that was earlier developed by the two master’s thesis students, ideas about the features and scenarios to be integrated into the framework were presented and evaluated, thereafter, the most important features/scenarios were chosen for implementation.



Meeting Times

The development process in the VARobot project group is being organized with the use of agile Scrum methodology. The Scrum master/Project leader ensures that every member of the group has the right tools to work. The meetings are structured in the following way:

  • Internal Weekly Meeting: The meeting holds twice in a week. It is a meeting where the project group members get to meet amongst themselves. There is a stand up part where everyone explains what he/she achieved in the last cycle and there is also an open discussion about the order of tasks.
  • Bi-Weekly Meeting: This is when the project group members meet with the supervisors. Everyone presents what he/she achieved in the last cycle.
